**Rule one. Let Apple handle the bits vibe-coding gets wrong.** **Six reasons going iOS — with AI — is defensible.** **Distribution.** The App Store ships your work to real users. Search works. Trust is handled. **I've done zero marketing on Migrapulse and I'm getting paying customers.** **Monetisation.** Apple's payment rails. **No Stripe. No tax to wrangle.** They take fifteen to thirty percent — and they earn it. **Authorisation.** Sign in with Apple. **One line of code.** No password reset emails. **The exact bit AI rolls dangerously badly on the web — solved on iOS, for free.** **Privacy.** On-device storage. No server. **No 3 a.m. incident emails.** **Hardware.** HealthKit. Camera. Mic. Sensors. **Web can't touch any of it.** Native iOS unlocks a whole category of apps web simply can't ship. **On-device AI.** Apple Intelligence. Neural Engine. **Free. Fast. Private.** Run a model on the user's phone without a server bill. Web can't. **Don't go web because it feels easier.** The hard bits — the defensible bits — are already solved on iOS.

**Rule three. Design before you build.** Vibe-coding land says "skip design — just describe it and build." **Don't.** Design first. The breakthrough — **AI-specific design tools.** Not Figma. Not Sketch. **Tools built for AI to design with.** **UX Pilot** — my pick. Models tuned for design. **An entire end-to-end flow in an hour.** **Claude Design** — polish the screens. Refine by describing what you want. **Google Stitch** — generate from a brief. Iterate fast. **Here's the thing — this is where the most creativity happens.** If you just say "do me a voice logging thing" and vibe-code it quickly — **you're going to get the average.** That's what AI does. You're not going to get something novel. **You're not going to get something unique that stands out — that positions you differently — that makes your screenshots in the App Store look different from everyone else's.** **And different is a massive currency right now.** Especially when people are complaining everything looks the same. **Spend the time finding your vibe. Through vibing.** Let it all out. **Talk it out.** UX Pilot, Claude Design, Google Stitch — they're where you do that. This is where designers shine. Because we **describe well.** We **collect references.** We **have taste.** **AI design tools amplify exactly that.** And the magic — they all **export HTML.** When you hand the builder a screenshot it squints and guesses. When you hand it HTML it reads it like English. **You describe. They structure. AI reads it.** That's the chain.

**Rule four. Vibes in. Order out.** **I'm very pro vibes. Vibes are the input.** Talk it through. References, sketches, mood, the lot — offload everything. **What you don't do is leave it as vibes.** AI plays your vibes back as a structured document. **`instructions.md`.** Plain text. Lightly structured. **You review the black box.** Instead of AI doing god-knows-what behind a chat window, the plan is on the table. **You can read it. You can edit it. You can spot the wrong-headed bit before it's wired into a thousand lines of Swift.** For Migrapulse — **186 numbered build steps.** Each cross-references the user stories, screens, and data it touches. **Nothing gets invented. Nothing drifts.** And — **plan mode landing in every IDE is the industry catching up to exactly this.**

**Rule five. Understand your schema.** This is the rule that bites everyone. **AI will never ask "is this safe?"** AI is the friend who'll happily help you move house. **It won't check whether your insurance is up to date.** This is a real one-star review for a competitor's app. **"Latest update wiped 8 months of data."** A schema mistake shipped. The migration didn't account for an old field. On launch, the app saw it didn't match and **wiped the local store.** Eight months of someone's headache logs. **Gone.** Apple won't roll you back. There's no "undo" on a shipped iOS app. **Audit your save format before every release. Every release.** And — quick aside — **use a panel of AI reviewers.** Don't ask the chef to review their own cooking. Different agent for review than for build. Fresh context catches schema mistakes the builder talked itself past.
